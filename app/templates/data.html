Straw yield dataset


The straw dataset includes the average straw yield per hectares in some of the shires in Western Australia for 2006 to 2010 and the total residue in each shire. This has been sourced from the Australian Government National map. At the moment, data are available only for 25 shires in WA.

Soil pH dataset


The pH of the soil is a measure of acidity or alkalinity. It basically indicates how ‘sour or sweet’ the soil is. The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14, with 7 being neutral. Values below 7 indicate an acid soil, and above 7, alkaline. Soil pH determines the nutrient availability to plants. Soils are more alkaline near the limestone-based coastal sands. Soils further inland, and most agricultural areas, are naturally acidic. Typically, Western Australian soils have a pH range between 4 and 8.5, (https://www.agric.wa.gov.au/soil-ph-and-plant-health?page=0%2C0). This dataset is a CSV file from an Australian Government dataset which reports soil pH measured for 1315 samples collected across Australia, with relative coordinates of the station.

Rainfall dataset


Rainfall contributes to a soil acidity. Water (H₂O) combines with carbon dioxide (CO₂) to form a weak acid — carbonic acid (H₂CO₃). The weak acid ionizes, releasing hydrogen (H⁺) and bicarbonate (HCO₃). The released hydrogen ions replace the calcium ions held by soil colloids, causing the soil to become acidic. The displaced calcium (Ca⁺⁺) ions combine with the bicarbonate ions to form calcium bicarbonate, which, being soluble, is leached from the soil. The net effect is increased soil acidity, (https://www.cropnutrition.com/nutrient-management/soil-ph). Obviously, rain also contribute to reduce water consumption from aquifers and catchments, which are used to irrigate crops.

The rainfall dataset has been retrieved from the department of primary industries and regional development of Western Australia, from an API endpoint. The dataset consist of the total rain in mm collected in 226 stations in Western Australia. Coordinates, station name, datetime, rainfall period are also available within the dataset.
